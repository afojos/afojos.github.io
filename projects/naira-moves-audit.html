Overview

What Was Analysed & How

For 4 months — January through April 2026 — I tracked every single financial transaction across income, expenses, and savings using a custom Excel tracker I built. The data was then visualised into a 5-tab interactive dashboard covering Overview, Income, Expenses, Savings, and Budget — then a financial health score was calculated across 5 dimensions.

This was not a simulation. These are real transactions, real amounts, real gaps. The discomfort of the findings is what made them valuable.

Recommendations

6 Specific Actions to Fix This

01
Automate ₦40,000 savings on payday — before spending anything
Set up an automatic transfer the moment income arrives. Removes the decision entirely. Target: bring savings rate from 4.6% to 20% within 6 months.
02
Switch to an annual data bundle — target 50% reduction
Annual bundles cost 40–60% less than monthly top-ups. Frees up ₦8,000–₦12,000 per month directly into savings.
03
Hard cap entertainment at ₦8,000/month — cash envelope method
When the ₦8,000 is gone, it's gone. Saves ₦10,000+ monthly and immediately improves the 50/30/20 balance.
04
Emergency fund to ₦375,000 first — before all other savings goals
At 0.41 months coverage, one bad month creates a crisis. Prioritise this above investment and business capital goals.
05
Build one passive income stream by Q3 2026
Options: productise a service into a retainer, create a digital product, or invest in dividend instruments. Goal: ₦20,000/month passive by September 2026.
06
Adopt the 50/30/20 framework strictly from next payday
₦100,000 needs · ₦60,000 wants · ₦40,000 savings. Based on ₦200,000 monthly income. This is the framework — not a suggestion.
