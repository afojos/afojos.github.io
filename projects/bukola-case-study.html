Overview

What Was Analysed

The Bukola Fatunde dataset on Kaggle contains 807 real personal finance transactions — credits, debits, and running balances from a real Nigerian earner. I applied the Naira Moves financial analysis framework to this external dataset to prove the method is repeatable and not just self-analysis.

The dataset was cleaned, categorised, and run through ratio analysis — income, expense breakdown, savings rate, behavioural patterns by day and month, and a full financial health score using the same scoring model I built for my own data.

What 807 Real Transactions Revealed

80.6% Expense Ratio — Above the Danger Threshold

Significantly better than my own 4.6%. Despite the high expense ratio, this person maintained near-benchmark savings — suggesting income is large enough to absorb the expenses.

Dining Out = ₦812,400 — #1 Expense Category
20.6% of all spending went to dining out. Highest spike in September, particularly on Sundays and Mondays. This is the single biggest lever for improvement.

September Spending Spikes +34% Above Average
Every September, total spending jumps dramatically — mostly from dining. This is a predictable seasonal pattern that can be pre-empted with a monthly cap.
Seasonal

Home Improvement Always Lands on Thursdays and Fridays
Every home improvement purchase in the dataset occurs on Thu or Fri — a consistent unplanned pattern. Could be budgeted proactively with a monthly envelope.
Pattern

Weekends Are Surprisingly Quiet
Saturday and Sunday see below-average spending (excluding September dining). Contrary to expectation — this person's weekday habits drive their costs.
